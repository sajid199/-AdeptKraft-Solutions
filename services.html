Sourcing

Sourcing is the strategic process of identifying, engaging, and attracting potential candidates across diverse platforms and methodologies, leveraging job boards, grassroots initiatives, and social media job postings. It extends to specialized efforts in sourcing for Applicant Tracking Systems (ATSs) and Vendor Management Systems (VMSs), enabling streamlined talent acquisition for both contract and full-time roles. By integrating innovative tools and personalized outreach, sourcing bridges the gap between talent needs and organizational goals, ensuring a robust and dynamic candidate pipeline.

Screening

Screening is the process of evaluating candidates to determine their qualifications, suitability, and alignment with a job's requirements and an organization's needs. It involves reviewing resumes, conducting interviews, and assessing skills, experience, and cultural fit. Screening ensures a focused and efficient recruitment process by filtering out unqualified candidates and shortlisting those who best match the role's criteria for further consideration.

Recruitment Coordination/Expediting

Recruitment Coordination/Expediting is the structured process of managing and accelerating the operational and logistical aspects of the hiring lifecycle to ensure a seamless and efficient recruitment experience. This includes:

  • Scheduling and logistics
  • Document management
  • Stakeholder alignment
  • Process optimization
  • Expediting decision-making
  • Background verification and coordination

Contractual Hiring:

Contractual hiring refers to engaging employees on a fixed-term basis for specific projects or durations.

Employees are typically paid hourly, daily, or monthly based on agreed rates.

Payment may be handled directly by the employer or through third-party agencies

Taxes and benefits are often limited or not included, depending on the agreement.

Permanent Hiring

Permanent hiring involves onboarding employees for long-term roles with no predetermined end date.

Employees receive a fixed salary, usually paid monthly.

Includes additional benefits like health insurance, retirement plans, and paid leave.

Employers deduct taxes and provide statutory benefits as per local labor laws.

Resource-on-Demand

Resource-on-Demand (RoD) is a flexible staffing model where organizations access skilled professionals or teams on an as-needed basis to meet immediate project requirements, address skill gaps, or manage fluctuating workloads.
